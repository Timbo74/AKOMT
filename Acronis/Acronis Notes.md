## EDR / XDR / MDR

**Reference:** 
https://www.acronis.com/en/blog/posts/edr-mdr-xdr-everything-you-need-to-know/

**EDR (Endpoint Detection and Response)** focuses solely on monitoring and securing individual devices like laptops and servers.

**XDR (Extended Detection and Response)** expands EDR by gathering and correlating threat data across your entire environment—including endpoints, network traffic, and cloud services.

**MDR (Managed Detection and Response)** is an outsourced service where human experts monitor your security tools 24/7.



## PII, PHI, and PCI Data

 - PII identifies an individual
 - PHI covers patient medical records
 - PCI includes payment card details

Safeguarding these distinct datasets is critical to prevent breaches and avoid hefty regulatory fines.

### Personally Identifiable Information (PII)

PII encompasses any information that can be used to distinguish or trace an individual's identity.Examples: Full names, addresses, Social Security Numbers (SSN), driver's license numbers, email addresses, and static IP addresses.Regulations: Governed by broad privacy frameworks like the European Union's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and California's CCPA.

### Protected Health Information (PHI)

PHI is a specific subset of PII that relates to an individual's physical or mental health, healthcare services provided, or health-related billing.Examples: Medical records, treatment histories, test results, and insurance claims.Regulations: Heavily regulated in the United States by the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA).

### Payment Card Industry (PCI) Data

PCI (often referred to as PCI DSS) data relates strictly to payment cards, cardholders, and financial transactions.Examples: Primary Account Number (PAN), cardholder name, expiration dates, and Sensitive Authentication Data (SAD) like CVV codes.Regulations: Mandated by the global PCI Security Standards Council (PCI SSC) through the PCI Data Security Standard (PCI DSS).
